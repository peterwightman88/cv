Thought Projection Project



Working closely with a team of scientists and programmers from the University of California, NASA, the University of Austin Texas and ATR Computation Neurosciences of Kyoto Japan. I worked on a project that would project your dreams or thoughts onto a screen.


This was achieved by designing a system to take samples of electrical signals from the retina to the brain’s very own visual cortex. This is the human body’s way of projecting data images that you see into the brain. The team so far has involved simple trained images, taking samples from the brain’s activity and then finally reconstructing what the test subject’s eye are seeing.


This is the first time this has ever been achieved, although some people may argue that it has very far reaching implications. We do believe that eventually when we have enough understanding of decoding brain activity, that we will be able to project a lot more diverse imagery and thus be able to openly display what is on somebody’s mind. Including projecting dreams onto a screen.


Human rights activists argue that dreams are best left alone and should not be able to be projected. However, we feel that the fields of psychology and psychiatry could make very good use of this information. The criminal justice system could also massively benefit from being able to read people’s thoughts. Although this is a very touchy subject!
